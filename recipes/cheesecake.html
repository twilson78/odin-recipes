Game Day Oreo Cheesecake

Description

Ah, sweet, sweet victory! Even if your team doesn't win, scoring a piece of this cheesecake is victory enough!

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • Cooking spray for the pan
  • Package of Oreo cookies
  • 6 tablespoons of salted butter, melted

For the filling:

  • 4- 8 ounce packages cream cheese, at room temperature
  • 1 1/4 cups sugar
  • 1/2 cup sour cream, at room temperature
  • 2 Tbsp. all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp. kosher salt
  • 4 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 1 Tbsp. vanilla extract
  • 18 Oreos, cut into quarters
  • Boiling water, for the water bath

For the topping:

  • 4 ounces of bittersweet 60% chocolate, finely chopped
  • 1 1/4 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 3 Tbsp. sugar
  • 1/2 tsp. vanilla extract
  • 10 Oreos

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F. Wrap heavy-duty foil around the outside bottom and sides of a 9-inch springform pan. Spray the inside of the pan lightly with nonstick cooking spray.
  2. For the crust: Pulse the cookies in a food processor until crumbs are fine. Add in the melted butter and pulse until combined. Press the mixture into the bottom and 1 ¾ inches up the sides of the prepared pan. Bake until set and fragrant, 10 to 12 minutes. Let cool completely on a rack, about 30 minutes.
  3. For the filling: In a stand mixer with the paddle attachment, beat the cream cheese on medium speed until no lumps remain, about 2 minutes. Turn the mixer off and add the sugar, sour cream, flour, and salt. Beat on medium speed until the batter is smooth and creamy, about 1 minute, scraping down the sides and bottom of the bowl as needed.
  4. Reduce speed to low. Add in one egg at a time, mix until just combined after each addition. Continue scraping down the sides and bottom of the bowl as needed. Beat in the vanilla. Fold in the cookies by hand.
  5. Pour the filling into the crust in the pan and place it in a large roasting pan. Transfer the roasting pan to the oven and carefully add enough boiling water so that it comes about halfway up the side of the springform pan.
  6. Bake until the cheesecake is set on the sides and just barely jiggles in the center when the pan is shaken, 1 hour 10 minutes to 1 hour 20 minutes. Turn the oven off and crack open the oven door. Leave the cheesecake in the oven for 15 minutes.
  7. Carefully remove the cheesecake from the water bath and transfer it to a wire rack. Let it cool to room temperature, about 2 hours. Place in the refrigerator to chill completely, at least 8 hours.
  8. Run a thin knife along the edge of the springform pan before removing the collar from the pan. Transfer Cheesecake to a serving platter.
  9. For the topping: Place chocolate in a medium bowl. Microwave 1/2 cup of the heavy cream until steaming, using a microwave safe cup. Approximately 1-2 minutes, DO NOT let it boil. Carefully pour cream over chocolate, let set for a couple minutes and mix well.
  10. Pipe ganache over the egde of the cheesecake to allow it to drip down its sides. Do this around the cake as a decorative border. Pour the remaining ganache over the center and spread smooth with a spatula.
  11. In the stand mixer with a whisk attachment, beat the remaining 3/4 cup of heavy whipping cream until thickened. Add sugar and vanilla, beat on high until peaks form.
  12. Top on cheesecake, and Oreos before slicing and serving.
  13. Enjoy.
